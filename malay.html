Malay Cuisine

Nasi Lemak

Nasi Lemak is a quintessential Malaysian dish, often considered the country's national dish. It consists of fragrant rice cooked in coconut milk and pandan leaves, served with sambal, fried crispy anchovies, toasted peanuts, boiled eggs, and cucumber slices. Sometimes, it's accompanied by fried chicken, beef rendang, or squid.

Nasi Kerabu

Originating from Malaysia's Kelantan region, Nasi Kerabu is a blue rice dish colored with butterfly pea flowers. It’s served with fresh herbs, vegetables, salted fish, and a spicy sambal sauce.

❮ ❯

Gulai

Gulai is a rich and flavorful curry dish popular in Indonesia, Malaysia, and other parts of Southeast Asia. It has origins in Indonesian cuisine, influenced by Indian and Middle Eastern cooking traditions. Gulai typically features a variety of meats such as chicken, beef, or fish, simmered in a coconut milk-based sauce infused with a blend of spices like turmeric, coriander, cumin, and lemongrass.
