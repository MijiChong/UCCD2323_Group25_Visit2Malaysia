Others Cuisine

Kolo Mee

Hailing from the Chinese communities in Sarawak, Malaysia, Kolo Mee is a popular noodle dish known for its simplicity and flavor. The dish is made with springy egg noodles, typically tossed in a fragrant mix of lard, shallot oil, soy sauce, and sometimes vinegar. Toppings often include minced pork, char siu (barbecued pork), and chopped scallions, giving it a savory and slightly sweet taste.

Sarawak Laksa

Sarawak Laksa is a delicacy that originated from Sarawak. The main ingredient is sambal laksa which is made from various ingredients. Sarawak laksa is not in the same category as other laksa in Malaysia because Sarawak laksa uses shrimp and chicken broth.

Sinalau Bakas

Sinalau Bakas originates from the Tausug people of the Sulu Archipelago in the Philippines. This traditional dish features marinated wild boar, often grilled over an open flame for a smoky flavor. Ingredients include soy sauce, vinegar, garlic, and local spices. It's commonly served with a spicy vinegar dip, highlighting the rich and savory taste of the boar meat.

Fish Noodles

A traditional dish from Southeast Asia, particularly popular in Malaysia. The dish features noodles made from fish paste, often accompanied by a flavorful broth. Key ingredients include fish fillets, starch, and seasonings like garlic and ginger. The noodles are usually served in a savory soup with vegetables, mushrooms, and sometimes tofu, providing a rich and comforting meal.
