Malay Festival

Hari Raya Aidilfitri

Hari Raya Aidilfitri marks the end of Ramadan, the Islamic holy month of fasting. It is a time of forgiveness, family gatherings and feasting. Malaysians dress in traditional clothing, offer prayers at mosques and visit the graves of loved ones. The celebration often includes the open house tradition where friends and family are invited to share meals.

Hari Raya Haji

Hari Raya Haji commemorates the willingness of Prophet Ibrahim to sacrifice his son in obedience to God. Muslims in Malaysia perform the Qurban ritual, where livestock is sacrificed and the meat is distributed to the poor. It is also a day for communal prayers and family gatherings.

Chinese Festival

Chinese New Year

Chinese New Year is the most important festival in Chinese culture. It marks the beginning of the lunar new year and is celebrated with family reunions, feasting, and various customs to bring good fortune. Traditional practices include giving red envelopes (angpau) filled with money, setting off firecrackers and performing the lion or dragon dance.

Lantern Festival

The Lantern Festival marks the end of the Chinese New Year celebrations. It is a time when families gather and appreciate the beauty of lantern displays. In some places, lantern parades and dragon or lion dances are also held. The festival symbolizes unity, completeness, and the return of spring.

Mid-Autumn Festival

The Mid-Autumn Festival is a time for family reunions and is associated with the legend of Chang'e, the Moon Goddess. It is celebrated by admiring the full moon, lighting lanterns and eating mooncakes, a special pastry filled with sweet or savory ingredients. The festival is marked by lantern processions and mooncake tasting events.

Dragon Boat Festival

Dragon Boat Festival commemorates the life and death of the famous Chinese scholar and poet Qu Yuan. It is celebrated with dragon boat races where teams paddle in sync to the beat of drums and by eating zongzi, which are sticky rice dumplings wrapped in bamboo leaves.

Indian Festival

Deepavali

Deepavali is one of the most important Hindu festivals. During Deepavali, homes are decorated with oil lamps (diyas), rangoli (colored patterns on the floor) and fireworks. Families gather to perform prayers (puja), usually to the goddess Lakshmi, the deity of wealth and share sweets and gifts.

Thaipusam

Thaipusam is a significant Hindu festival observed primarily by the Tamil community. It honors Lord Murugan, the Hindu god of war and involves acts of devotion, penance and thanksgiving. Devotees carry kavadis or pierce their bodies as a form of penance and carry offerings to the temple.

Others Festival

Kaamatan

Kaamatan is celebrated by the Kadazan-Dusun and other indigenous groups in Sabah. It is a festival that marks the end of the rice harvesting season. It is a time to give thanks to the spirits, particularly the rice spirit, Bambarayon, for a bountiful harvest.

GawaiDayak

Gawai Dayak is celebrated by the Iban and other Dayak communities in Sarawak. It is a harvest festival, marking the end of the rice harvesting season. It is a time to thank the gods and spirits for a successful harvest and to ask for blessings for the coming farming season.
