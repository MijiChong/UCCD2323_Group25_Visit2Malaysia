Chinese Cuisine

Char Kway Teow

Originating from Southeast Asia, Char Kway Teow is a popular street food in Malaysia. It consists of flat rice noodles stir-fried with prawns, Chinese sausage, eggs, bean sprouts, and chives, often in a dark soy sauce with chili.

❮ ❯

Bak Kut Teh

A traditional Chinese herbal soup from Malaysia, Bak Kut Teh literally means "meat bone tea." It consists of pork ribs simmered with garlic, soy sauce, and a mix of herbs and spices such as star anise, cinnamon, and cloves.

❮ ❯

Dim Sum

A Cantonese cuisine tradition, Dim Sum refers to a variety of bite-sized dishes typically served in small steamer baskets or on plates such as Har Gow, Siu Mai. Ingredients vary widely but often include shrimp, pork, vegetables, and dough in forms like dumplings, buns, and rolls.
